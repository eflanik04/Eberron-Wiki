Physical manifestations of the Draconic Prophecy that appear as unique, natural marks on certain bloodlines.







Creation

Dragonmarks are physical manifestations of the Draconic Prophecy, and typically appear on certain bloodlines of Khorvaire. They can appear at random, sometimes creating an Aberrant Dragonmark.

Appearance

Dragonmarks are physical marks, more intricate than a birthmark and more distinct than a tattoo. As the wearer ages, and the dragonmark gains power, it will start to expand in size and complexity while keeping the same general shape and idea. Dragonmarks vary in patterns and color, depending on the house and the wearer.

Types of Marks

There are four different types of marks. Lesser, Greater, Siberys, and Aberrant.

Lesser Marks

Lesser marks are typically the first marks to appear when a creature reaches adolescence. These are the simplest forms of the mark, and offer the lowest form of magic. These can be studied and focused on to create Greater Marks.

Greater Marks

Greater marks are often results of an expanding and growing lesser mark. These marks offer a greater power, with less understanding. Most average mark wearers will reach their full potential with a greater mark.

Siberys Marks

Siberys marks are the rarest mark, and the final form of a dragonmark. Most mark wearers never reach the Siberys evolution, and those who do are said to be descendants of Siberys himself.

Aberrant Marks

Aberrant dragonmarks can appear on any race, and are the most dangerous mark form. The most common way to manifest an aberrant mark is for two different dragonmarked families to have a child. This union is forbidden by The Twelve.

History

Before the formation of Galifar, aberrant marks were much more common. The first Sharn was destroyed with an earthquake by the powers of an aberrant leader. In an effort to eradicate dangerous individuals, the dragonmarked houses united in the War of the Mark and hunted down all aberrant marks, significantly reducing the numbers of those which such marks. Present day, aberrants are no longer hunted down and killed, but most dragonmarks fear the aberrants, and will not hesitate to take them out.

The Dragonmarks

Mark of Detection

Associated with the House Medani
The mark of detection provides the wearer with the ability to detect traps and possible dangers.

  • Lesser Properties: When you make an Investigation or Insight check, you can roll 1d4 and add the number rolled to the ability check. You always have the Detect Magic spell prepared, and can cast it once per day without expending a spell slot. You can also use an appropriate spell slot to cast this spell.
  • Greater Properties: You always have the Detect Poison and Disease spell prepared, and can cast it once per day without expending a spell slot. You can also use an appropriate spell slot to cast this spell. At level 3, you have access to See Invisibility and can cast it in the same way. You have advantage on Investigation and Insight checks.

Mark of Finding

Associated with the House Tharashk
The mark of Finding grants the wearer abilities related to locating people and objects.

  • Lesser Properties: When you make a Perception or Survival check, you can roll 1d4 and add the number rolled to the ability check. You always have the Hunters Mark spell prepared, and can cast it once per day without expending a spell slot. You can also use an appropriate spell slot to cast this spell.
  • Greater Properties: You have Date Object always prepared, and can cast it once per day without expending a spell slot. You can also use an appropriate spell slot to cast this spell. At level 3 you have access to Locate Object, and can cast it in the same way. You have advantage on Perception and Survival checks.
