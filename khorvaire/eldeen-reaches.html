Exclusive to Eldeen Reaches

The Gloaming

The Gloaming covers the southmost part of Eldeen Reaches, technically in the The Towering Wood. This area is separated into 3 rings, the central intersection being the most powerful part of the forest.

The Towering Wood

The Towering Wood is a dense, ancient forest home to the many tribes of shifters and Druidic Circles. This forest is vast, and mainly untamed. The Wardens of the Wood operate out of this forest, but only out of a small portion. The fey presence is strong here, often creating manifest zones tied to Thelanis.

The Twilight Demesne

Located in the northwest corner of The Towering Wood, The Twilight Demesne is a manifest zone for the realm of Thelanis.

Guardian Trees

The heart of the Towering Wood, these awakened trees are large, and tower over all the others. It is believed that these trees were the first trees ever made on Eberron.

History

Druids and shifters had called the region of the Eldeen Reaches home for thousands of years. Originally, the eastern part of the Reaches was claimed by Aundair. However, during The Last War, Aundair reduced their focus of this area, ignoring the banditry plaguing the farmers inhabiting the land. Eventually the druids came to the aid of these farmers, causing the people of eastern Aundair to secede and form The Eldeen Reaches, a nation that would be recognized by the Treaty of Thronehold

Civilization

There are two main groups of civilizations located in Eldeen Reaches. These are the Druid Sects to the west and agriculture groups to the east.

Alvirad

A small settlement, roughly 3,000 in population. The settlers here are mostly farmers and argiculturers.

Cree

Sitting on the Lake Galifar, Cree serves as both a fishing and agriculture settlement. Most fishing boats can be seen from the other side of Lake Galifar, as the fishermen of Cree are a fearless crew who venture to the middle of the lake.

Delethorn

On the Western shore of Lake Galifar, Delethorn hosts House Orien trade roads. The House Vadalis has an enclave here, and has much influence in this town that they essentially control it. This town does commerce with most of Khorvaire, and is host to the more exotic dealings of House Vadalis.

Erlaskar

This settlement sits on the northern coast of the Silver Lake. Just like Delethorn, Erlaskar also acts as a sophisticated beast trading and training settlement from House Vadalis.

Greenblade

Greenblade a small, 5 family town that primarily grows crops for House Vadalis to feed their animals.

Greenheart

The Captial of Eldeen Reaches, and the base of operations for the Wardens of the Wood. Greenheart is the grove of Great Druid Oalian, the awakened greatpine. Greenheart is often a place of negotiation, where the many druid sects will send embassadors to settle disputes. The population is about 1,260, however often will grow during druidic ceremonies or time of worship. Commerce does not exist in Greenheart, as they do not believe in it. The town has no marketplace or tavern. They mostly rely on local hunters and tradesmith within the community.

Varna

The largest settlement in Eldeen Reaches, with a population of 8,000. House Vadalis operates out of Varna, and serves as the primary commerce and trade center between Eldeen Reaches and Aundair and other nations. This city is naturally built with Aundarian architecture and structure. There are many stables and farms for housing and breeding various beasts, mostly owned by House Vadalis. The commerce and trade is dominated by the sale of beasts and exotic animals, mostly from House Vadalis. Varna also hosts small enclaves for the other Dragonmark Houses.

The Hunters Grove

A massive temple to Balinor and The Sovereign Host, set in Varna.

Wolf's Paw

Wolf's Paw lies in the rural northeast of the Reaches. It is entirely populated by shifters who live an agricultural lifestyle. This city has ties to Wardens of the Wood.

Wardens of the Wood

One of the many druidic sects based in The Towering Wood. They are tasked with upholding order and settling disputes. They seek to protect travelers, however much of the Wood is still untouched by any race.

Ashbound

An extreme druidic order who consider all arcane and divine magic outside of druidic magic to be unnatural. They seek to protect wildlife and consider most aspects of modern society to be a threat to the wildlands. They ideally avoid bloodshed, but some more extreme members consider acts of terrorism to prove their point.

Children of Winter

This small druidic sect heavily embraces death and decay as a part of the natural cycle. These druids seek to preserve the cycle of life and death, and restore the balance between the two. They are willing to use disease and death to clear out the week in order to create room for new life. They seek to either prevent or advance a coming cataclysm they call "Winter". The majority of the population that makes up the Children of Winter are humans, the rest being shifters. These druids are fascinating with the Day of Mourning, and left the The Towering Wood to study it, and aid in bringing the age to an end. The Children base out of The Gloaming.

Gatekeepers

The Gatekeepers are the oldest druid sect in Khorvaire, mostly made up of Orcs, half-orcs, and later shifters. These druids were heavily decimated during the invasions of the Daelkyr. Their main task was to defend nature against aberrations, outsiders, undead, and other unnatural beings.

Greensingers

A small druidic sect that maintains relations with the fey of the Eldeen Reaches. These druids can typically be found near the Twilight Demesne
